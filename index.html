Dr. Fergus Hamilton

NIHR Clinical Lecturer in Infectious Diseases

Clinical academic focused on infection outcomes, genomics, and trial design at the University of Bristol. H-index 33, co-leading national trials and translating evidence into policy.

About

I am an NIHR Clinical Lecturer at the University of Bristol, specialising in severe infection, host genomics, and causal inference to improve outcomes. My work spans large pragmatic trials, mechanistic studies, and rapid-response public health research, with a focus on translating evidence into practice and policy.

Key contributions include COVID-19 outcomes research (DISCOVER), aerosol transmission studies (AERATOR), and genetic analyses of iron and infection. I currently co-lead major NIHR-funded trials, including ASPECT (aspirin in pneumonia) and DEXACELL (dexamethasone in cellulitis).

Research Focus

Trials & Translational Research

Co-applicant on ASPECT (NIHR HTA, £2.1m, >3,000 participants) evaluating aspirin in pneumonia, and co-lead for DEXACELL (NIHR HTA, £1.5m, recruitment ahead of target).

COVID-19 Outcomes

Co-led DISCOVER (£0.3m), delivering the first UK description of long-term outcomes after hospitalised COVID-19 and evidence for vaccine effects on long COVID.

Aerosol & Infection Control

Study coordinator for AERATOR (NIHR-UKRI Urgent Public Health, £0.6m), demonstrating cough risk exceeding many AGPs and informing NHS England’s AGP review.

Host Genomics & Immunity

PhD research on iron and infection risk, HMOX1 functional variants, and Mendelian randomisation insights on IL-6 signalling and infectious disease susceptibility.

Leadership & Mentorship

Team Development

Supervised clinical academic trainees and NIHR Associate PIs across multiple trials, supporting successful fellowships, publications, and academic appointments.

Mentoring Impact

Guided early-career researchers to competitive NIHR awards and first-author publications, with ongoing collaborations on NIHR-funded trials.

Recognition

Recipient of the Royal College of Pathology’s Gold Medal (2023) for infection control research and national policy impact.

Research Community & Service

Methods & Standards

Published methodological evaluations of non-linear Mendelian randomisation, clarifying when the method can produce biased findings.

Guideline Leadership

Founding steering group member for WikiGuidelines and author on multiple international clinical guidelines.

Editorial Roles

Associate Editor for the Journal of Infection and reviewer for Nature Communications, BMJ, and Lancet specialty journals.

Clinical Governance

Chair of the ALSPAC clinical advisory group, overseeing clinical decision-making for participant results.

Societal & Patient Impact

Patient partnership is central to my research. For DEXACELL, we co-designed inclusive recruitment for people who use intravenous drugs, including tailored follow-up and patient-facing materials highlighted by the NIHR HTA panel.

I currently lead the James Lind Alliance Priority Setting Partnership in pneumonia, bringing patients, clinicians, and researchers together to guide future funding. For AERATOR, we created a comic strip explaining aerosol findings to patients, featured on the front cover of Thorax and cited in recognition of the study’s impact.
